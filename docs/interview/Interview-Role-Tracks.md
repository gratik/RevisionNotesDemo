## Interview Answer Block

- 30-second answer: Role tracks help prioritize preparation by mapping expectations to the most relevant modules.
- 2-minute deep dive: I pick a target role, focus on priority modules, and practice concise outcome-based answers tied to measurable impact.
- Common follow-up: How do you adapt when applying for multiple roles?
- Strong response: Keep a shared core prep set, then add role-specific deep dives and examples.
- Tradeoff callout: Over-customizing too early can fragment prep and reduce repetition quality.

## Interview Bad vs Strong Answer

- Bad answer: "I study everything equally and hope the role fit is obvious."
- Strong answer: "I map role expectations to focused modules, practice role-specific examples, and validate readiness with timed drills and feedback."
- Why strong wins: It shows deliberate prioritization and evidence-based preparation.

## Interview Timed Drill

- Time box: 10 minutes.
- Prompt: Choose one role and explain your 7-day prep plan with module priorities and measurable outcomes.
- Required outputs:
  - One prioritized module sequence
  - One role-specific risk and mitigation
  - One readiness metric
- Self-check score (0-3 each): role fit clarity, tradeoff quality, communication clarity.
